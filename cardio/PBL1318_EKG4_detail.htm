PBL 1318 EKG, at 3 Years

Note the bursts or salvos of a fast irregular rhythm that each spontaneously terminates.  When they terminate, there is resumption of sinus or atrial activity with a P wave.  Twice the time for the sinus node to recover from the fast rhythm is very long, causing a long "sinus pause."  Then each time, sinus rhythm is overdriven by another burst of atrial fibrillation.  Some P waves are normal (red arrows) but after the longer pauses, they are different (blue arrows) and may be an atrial escape beat.
