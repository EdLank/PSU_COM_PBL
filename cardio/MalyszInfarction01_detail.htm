Acute Myocardial Infarct, Transmural, Gross

Unaffected myocardium is "beefy" red (N).  A recent transmural infarct affects half the circumference of the left ventricle, from the endocardial surface to the epicardial surface.  It is brown, looking like broiled beef (asterisks).  There is "marbled" myocardium from an old healed infarct, with white scar within areas of more normal myocardium (left arrowhead).
