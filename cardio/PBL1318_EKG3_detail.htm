PBL 1318 EKG, at 9 Months

The EKG recorded on presentation to the Emergency Department.  Notice it shows a regular, fast rhythm, with narrow QRS complexes.  The atrial activity is hard to find.  Definite P waves cannot be seen, so the rhythm is not clear.  It is most generically a supraventricular tachycardia (SVT), but with this fast heart rate, T waves can obscure atrial activity.  Any maneuver that slows the heart rate can be helpful in diagnosing the rhythm.

Click only after the therapeutic intervention  ...and the rhythm is known.
