The yellow arrows show the edge of the lung (pleural margin) separated from the chest wall.  There are no lung markings peripheral to the retracted serosal pleural margin.  The red arrow shows an “air-fluid level” with fluid (in this case, blood) below the meniscus, and air above it.  The left costophrenic angle is still sharp, indicating that there is no fluid in that hemothorax.  Since the air/fluid interface is horizontal, the patient is upright, because gravity is creating the horizontal interface.
