Retina Roth Spots

On this retinal image, there are several Roth spots which are red spots marked by blue arrows.  The white center area yellow arrows shows the white central area.  Studies show the central area is white from fibrin thrombus, platelet aggregates, and infiltrating red blood cells at the site of a previous vessel rupture.  For orientation, the fovea, with the most dense optic cones, and the best visual acuity, is marked with the grey arrow.   The optic cup is the entry of the optic nerve into the posterior eye green arrow.
