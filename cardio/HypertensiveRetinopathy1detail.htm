Hypertensive Retinopathy

On this retinal image, the macula is marked by the asterisk.  In its center is the fovea, with the most dense optic cones, and the best visual acuity.
The hypertensive abnormalities visible are:
 The artery (small magenta arrow) and the vein (large magenta arrow) both show more prominent than normal light reflex (called "silver wiring").
 The optic disc margin (large black arrows) is indistinct, a sign of papiledema.
 There are retinal hemorrhages (large yellow arrowsblue arrow), and hard exudates (small yellow arrows).
 A-V nicking can occur where a paired artery and vein cross, and are encased in a vascular sheath.  The enlarged stiff artery causes the vein to be constrained, and at their crossing the vein is pinched  (small black arrows).
