CXR Case 2

This is a tension pneumothorax.  Notice the collapse of the lung away from the chest wall (serosal pleural edge at the blue arrow).  There is increased air pressure in the pleural space, showing it is a tension pneumothorax by
  1) the depressed right hemidiaphragm (at the yellow arrow)
  2) the deviation of the mediastinum to the left
  3) the shift of the mediastinum and esophagus (the nasogastric tube {red arrow} within the esophagus, is shifted to the left of the midline
  4) the unequal splaying of the ribs (the distance between ribs on the right is greater than on the left).
