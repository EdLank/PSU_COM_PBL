PBL 1311 Chest CT comparison
The normal chest CT is on the left, in about the same plane.  Note that this image is after an injection of IV contrast in the left arm, still visible in the L subclavian and L brachiocephalic vein.  That has made the pulmonary arteries more prominent, especially the left lower pulmonary artery.  The dramatic difference between the images is a lack of lymph nodes around the carina and the lower trachea and proximal bronchi.
In comparison, the right CT shows very large peri-tracheal and peri-bronchial masses in the same plane.
